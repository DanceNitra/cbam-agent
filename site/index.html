The Problem

CBAM reporting is complex, manual, and carries serious financial penalties for errors.

📊

Complex Emission Calculations

Each product category (cement, steel, aluminium, fertilisers, electricity, hydrogen) has different CN codes, emission factors, and calculation methodologies. Getting them wrong means penalties.

📅

Quarterly Deadlines

Reports are due every quarter under the permanent regime (since April 2026). Miss a deadline and you face €10-50/tCO₂e penalties plus potential exclusion from importing.

🌍

Multi-Country Data

Different countries have different carbon prices, default emission factors, and verification requirements. Tracking all 20+ major supplier countries is manual spreadsheet hell.

💰

Expensive Consultants

Big 4 firms charge €30K-€100K/year for CBAM advisory. Mid-size importers with 50-200 shipments/quarter can't justify that cost but can't afford errors either.

Regulatory Timeline

CBAM enforcement is accelerating. Here's what's happened and what's coming.

October 2023

Transitional Phase Begins

First CBAM quarterly reporting obligations begin. Reporting-only, no financial obligations.

January 2026

Permanent Regime Launches

CBAM certificates become mandatory. Importers must purchase certificates matching embedded emissions.

April 2026

First Q1 Report Under Permanent Regime

First quarterly report with real financial obligations. Deadline: end of April 2026.

May 2026

First Certificate Surrender Deadline

Importers must surrender certificates corresponding to Q1 2026 embedded emissions.

2034

Full Phase-Out of Free Allowances

EU ETS free allowances fully phased out. CBAM covers 100% of carbon cost.
